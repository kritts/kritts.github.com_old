Broad Institute of Harvard and MIT, Bioinformatics Research Platform

I spent my summer during 2012 in Boston, Massachusetts. A portion of my time during this period was spent at the Broad Institute where I worked on annotations for entries for the development of the BioAssay Research Database (BARD).

BARD is a large cross-center initiative funded by the NIH that is focused on creating a database to hold published data about chemicals and their biological activities for researchers and those in the pharmaceutical sector. It aims to provide researchers with well-organized and fully-annotated data and innovative tools to accelerate drug discovery.
